Life of Nelson Mandela

Nelson Mandela was a great leader, campaigner for justice, and an icon of democracy. Born in 1918 in South Africa, he became actively involved in the anti-apartheid movement, and was arrested and imprisoned in 1962. He was released in 1990, and in 1994 became the first black president of South Africa. He passed away in 2013.

Achievements

  • Nobel Peace Prize (1993)
  • Order of Meritorious Service (1992)
  • Order of Good Hope (1996)
  • Order of St John (1997)
  • Order of the Companions of OR Tambo (2001)

Legacy

Nelson Mandela is remembered as an inspiring leader, who fought for justice and equality in South Africa. He is remembered as an icon of democracy, and is an inspiration to people all over the world.
